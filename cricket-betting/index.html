International Cricket Council (ICC) Events:

  • ICC Cricket World Cup: this ODI (One Day International) tournament is held every four years, featuring national teams in a global battle for supremacy. It’s one of the most watched sporting events worldwide.
  • ICC World Test Championship: This relatively new format (inaugural edition in 2019) determines the best Test team across a two-year cycle. Matches from bilateral Test series between ICC nations contribute points, culminating in a final between the top two teams.
  • ICC T20 World Cup: This limited-over tournament, held every two years, features national teams competing in the exciting Twenty20 format. It’s known for its fast-paced, action-packed matches, makes it the most loved World Cup for youngsters.

Domestic League Tournaments:

  • Indian Premier League (IPL):  The most popular cricket league globally, the IPL is a Twenty20 tournament featuring city-based franchises in India. With a glitzy player auction, high-profile sponsorships, and top international cricketers, the IPL is a commercial juggernaut.
  • Big Bash League (BBL): Australia’s domestic Twenty20 league, the BBL, is known for its innovative format and fast-paced matches. It features city-based franchises and is a major source of entertainment for Australian cricket fans.
  • Pakistan Super League (PSL): Similar to the IPL and BBL, the PSL is a Twenty20 league featuring franchises in Pakistan. It has grown in popularity in recent years, attracting international stars and generating excitement among Pakistani cricket fans.
  • The Ashes: This historic Test series between England and Australia is one of the fiercest rivalries in cricket. Dating back to 1882, the Ashes holds immense prestige and is a highlight of the international cricket calendar.
